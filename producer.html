Producer - Presenter - Writer

Presenter
Patrick Johns' Jazz Night

Patrick has been a freelance producer/presenter since 2007, following seven years at BBC Radio 3, working in production across programmes such as Jazz Record Requests, Hear & Now, In Tune, Voices, and Late Junction.

He writes and presents the Music Education podcast Teaching Notes, and is the UK correspondent for ABC Overnights on Australian national radio.

Patrick wrote and co-presented an episode of BBC Radio 3’s The Early Music Show, on the subject of Catch Clubs and Glee Clubs of the 17th and 18th Centuries, and also presented the show “Patrick Johns’ Jazz Night” on internet radio.

Writer and co-presenter
The Early Music Show BBC Radio 3

Patrick has produced numerous documentaries and music shows for BBC Radio 2, including Jazz Junctions (2010), Country Christmas (2016) and the highly-acclaimed Top Brass (2018), presented by James Morrison.

Patrick has been interviewed on live radio by many of the UK’s top presenters, including Chris Evans, Danny Baker, Sean Keaveny, and Stuart Maconie.

Live on
Chris Evans' Drive Time BBC Radio 2

As a script-writer, Patrick worked for several years on Oman Classics, and the BBC Jazz Awards, as well as writing scripts for Radio 2, Radio 3 and for Qantas Airways in-house jazz documentaries, and he has had the pleasure of interviewing some of the top names in music including Burt Bacharach, McCoy Tyner, Annie Ross, Buddy DeFranco, Emilio Castillo and Jimmy Pankow.
