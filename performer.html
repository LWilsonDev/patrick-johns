Musician

Patrick is very active as a music arranger, in many different styles, and his arrangements have been heard on BBC 1, ITV and Channel 4, as well as Radios 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6, and in major London venues such as the Royal Festival Hall.

Arranging

  • Large Brass Ensemble: soon to be published by Superbrass Publishing
  • Children’s voices: arrangements for young voices published by Sing Up
  • Orchestra: in 2017, Patrick orchestrated 20 Elvis Presley songs, for “Symphonic Elvis” concerts given by the world’s no.1 Elvis tribute, Gordon Hendricks
  • Orchestra: in 2014, Patrick arranged and performed an orchestral version of Bohemian Rhapsody with Rainer Hersch, the London Firebird Orchestra, and the Crouch End Festival Chorus, at the Royal Festival Hall.
  • Choir: Last Choir Standing (BBC 1); The Mad Bad Ad Show (Channel 4)
  • Transcribing Africa (by Toto), and Since You Been Gone (recorded by Rainbow) for use as educational resources

Recent compositions include:

  • Mary Speaks: a cappella carol for SSA or TTB
  • In the Bleak Midwinter: carol for SA and piano
  • We Will Remember Them: Remembrance Day piece for SATB and orchestra (premiered in November 2018 at Bristol Cathedral’s Festival of Remembrance)
  • September Groove: for brass band (written for the charity Brass for Africa)

Patrick has been a professional trombonist for 20 years, playing in a wide variety of ensemble, either as a regular member or as a guest, including:

  • Oompah Brass, the UK’s leading Oktoberfest band and leading exponents of Oompop, including two 5* sell-out shows at the Edinburgh Fringe
  • London Vegetable Orchestra, playing 1st Butternut Squash, as recently seen on Ant and Dec’s Saturday Night Takeaway
  • Prince Buster (guest)
  • Chas ’n’ Dave (guest)
  • Hayseed Dixie (guest)
  • The Noblemen and Gentlemen’s Catch Club (professional member)
  • The Symphony Orchestra of India (principal trombone)
  • The Dualers (including two top 25 hits)
